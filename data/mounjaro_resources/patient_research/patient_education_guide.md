# Mounjaro (Tirzepatide) - Patient Education Guide

## What is Mounjaro?

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) is a prescription medicine that helps:
- **Adults with Type 2 Diabetes** improve blood sugar control
- **Adults with Obesity/Overweight** (as Zepbound) manage weight

It works by mimicking two natural hormones in your body: GLP-1 and GIP.

### Key Effects:
1. **Stimulates insulin release** when blood sugar is high
2. **Reduces appetite** and food cravings
3. **Slows digestion** so you feel full longer
4. **Decreases liver glucose** production

## Injection Instructions

### Preparation
1. **Wash hands** with soap and water
2. **Check the pen** - medicine should be clear and colorless to slightly yellow
3. **Do NOT use** if cloudy, discolored, or has particles
4. **Do NOT freeze** - if frozen, do not use

### Step-by-Step Injection

1. **Attach a new needle** to the pen
2. **Prime the pen** (first use only) - dial to prime dose, point up, push button
3. **Select your dose** by turning the dial
4. **Clean injection site** with alcohol wipe
5. **Insert needle** at 90° angle
6. **Push the dose button** and hold down
7. **Count slowly to 5** before removing
8. **Dispose needle safely** in a sharps container

## Dosing Schedule

### Titration (Dose Escalation)

| Weeks | Dose | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1-4 | 2.5 mg | Starting dose (not for glucose control) |
| 5-8 | 5 mg | First therapeutic dose |
| 9-12 | 7.5 mg | Escalation (if needed) |
| 13-16 | 10 mg | Escalation (if needed) |
| 17-20 | 12.5 mg | Escalation (if needed) |
| 21+ | 15 mg | Maximum dose |

**Important:** Your doctor may keep you at any dose that works for you.

### When to Inject
- **Once weekly** - same day each week
- **Any time of day** - with or without food
- **Flexible scheduling** - can change days (wait at least 3 days/72 hours between doses)

## Common Side Effects

### Most Common (Usually Improve Over Time)

| Side Effect | What to Do |
|-------------|------------|
| Nausea | Eat smaller meals, avoid fatty/fried foods |
| Diarrhea | Stay hydrated, eat bland foods |
| Decreased appetite | Eat smaller, nutrient-rich meals |
| Vomiting | Take small sips of water, rest |
| Constipation | Increase fiber and water intake |
| Stomach pain | Eat slowly, avoid trigger foods |

**Tip:** Most GI side effects occur during dose increases and get better over time.

---

## When to Call Your Doctor

### Seek Medical Attention If You Experience:

⚠️ **Severe stomach pain** that won't go away
⚠️ **Signs of allergic reaction** - rash, itching, swelling, trouble breathing
⚠️ **Symptoms of pancreatitis** - severe abdominal pain radiating to back
⚠️ **Vision changes** - especially if you have diabetic retinopathy
⚠️ **Signs of low blood sugar** - shakiness, sweating, confusion, fast heartbeat
⚠️ **Kidney problems** - decreased urination, swelling

## Storage Instructions

| Location | Temperature | Duration |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Refrigerator | 2°C to 8°C (36°F to 46°F) | Until expiration date |
| Room temperature (after first use) | Up to 30°C (86°F) | Up to 30 days |

**Do NOT:**
- Freeze the medicine
- Leave in direct sunlight
- Store in extreme heat (car in summer)
- Use after expiration date

## Lifestyle Tips for Best Results

### Diet
- Focus on protein and vegetables
- Eat smaller, more frequent meals
- Limit fried and fatty foods
- Stay hydrated

### Exercise
- Aim for 150 minutes/week of moderate activity
- Include strength training 2x/week
- Start slowly and build up

### Monitoring
- Track your weight weekly
- Monitor blood sugar if diabetic
- Keep a food journal if helpful
- Note any side effects to discuss with doctor
